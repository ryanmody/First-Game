You wake up in a cramped, grimy bathroom. The tiles beneath you are cold and cracked, and the smell of mildew hangs thick in the air. Your reflection in the broken mirror offers no answers, just a haggard face with tired eyes staring back at you. The sink is filled with stagnant water, and empty pill bottles are scattered across the counter.

The flickering light above casts eerie shadows on the walls, making the room feel even more oppressive. Mold crawls up the corners, and the ceiling drips steadily onto the peeling linoleum floor. The air is damp, and every breath feels heavy and laborious. You notice a rusty razor blade near the sinks, along with what looks like empty prescription bottles at first glance.

Although you try your best to comb through your memories, you are unable to find even the tiniest memory of who you are or what you were doing. Panic starts to set in as you confront the uneasy truth:

You don't know who you are or how you got here.
